The March family was very poor, but they were happy. There were four sisters named Meg, Jo, Beth, and Amy. Their father was away at war, and their mother worked hard to take care of them. It was almost Christmas, and the girls were sad because there would be no presents this year. They decided to use their own money to buy gifts for their mother instead of things for themselves. On Christmas morning, they woke up to find little books under their pillows. Their mother had managed to get them each a gift after all. Later that day, they shared their breakfast with a poor family who had nothing to eat. The girls learned that giving to others can bring more joy than getting presents.
